Robert R. Spano (born 27 August 1972) is an Icelandic/Italian jurist and professor of law, having also served as Parliamentary Ombudsman of <a href="/wiki/Iceland">Iceland</a>, ad hoc Judge of the EFTA Court in Luxembourg and Dean of the Faculty of Law, University of Iceland. On 25 June 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Assembly_of_the_Council_of_Europe">Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe</a> elected Professor Spano a Judge of the <a href="/wiki/European_Court_of_Human_Rights">European Court of Human Rights</a> with respect to <a href="/wiki/Iceland">Iceland</a>, his nine year term commencing on November 1, 2013. He received an absolute majority of votes cast by parliamentarians.
-----
Early life and Education.
Judge Spano was born in <a href="/wiki/Reykjav%C3%ADk">Reykjavík</a> on 27 August 1972. He graduated with a <a href="/wiki/Candidate_of_Law">Candidatus Juris</a> degree from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Iceland">University of Iceland</a> in 1997 and a <a href="/wiki/Magister_Juris">Magister Juris</a> degree in European and <a href="/wiki/Comparative_law">Comparative Law</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford">University of Oxford</a> (University College) on August 5, 2000. At Oxford, Spano received the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clifford_Chance_Prize&action=edit&redlink=1">Clifford Chance Prize</a> for best overall performance in the <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Laws">Magister Juris</a> Program (proxime accessit) and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Procedure_Prize&action=edit&redlink=1">Civil Procedure Prize</a>.
-----
Career.
In September 1997, Spano was appointed Deputy District Court Judge in the <a href="/wiki/District_Court">District Court</a> of <a href="/wiki/Reykjanes">Reykjanes</a>. In August 1998 he was employed as a legal adviser in the Office of the <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Ombudsman">Parliamentary Ombudsman</a> of <a href="/wiki/Iceland">Iceland</a> and was appointed Deputy to the Ombudsman in 2001, serving in that role until 2004. In 2002, Mr. Spano was appointed an assistant professor of law at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Iceland">University of Iceland</a> on a part-time basis, receiving tenure as an associate professor in 2004. In 2003, the Minister of Justice appointed Spano as Chairman of the Standing Committee of Experts in Criminal Law. In 2005, Professor Spano became Editor of the Law Review of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawyer%27s_Association_of_Iceland&action=edit&redlink=1">Lawyer's Association of Iceland</a>. In 2006, Professor Spano was awarded the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rector%27s_Prize&action=edit&redlink=1">Rector's Prize</a> for Excellence in Teaching. In November 2006, Spano was appointed a full professor of law in the fields of jurisprudence and public law and in September 2007 he was elected Vice-Dean of the Faculty of Law at the University of Iceland.
In 2007, the Prime Minister of Iceland asked Spano to chair a special commission of experts tasked with investigating human rights abuses in child care institutions from 1947-1992. The commission´s work took five years and produced several reports. In 2010, Spano was elected by the General Assembly of the National Church of Iceland to chair a commission of experts entrusted with investigating the reactions and procedures by the Church when allegations of sexual violence allegedly perpetrated by the Bishop of Iceland surfaced in 1996. The commission published its report in the summer of 2011.
On January 1st, 2009, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Praesidium_of_Parliament&action=edit&redlink=1">Praesidium of Parliament</a> appointed Professor Spano as <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Ombudsman">Parliamentary Ombudsman</a> of <a href="/wiki/Iceland">Iceland</a> in a provisional capacity as the elected Ombudsman, Mr. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tryggvi_Gunnarsson&action=edit&redlink=1">Tryggvi Gunnarsson</a>, was asked to serve in a special investigatory commission into the banking crises in Iceland. The role of the Ombudsman is to safeguard the rights of individuals in their relationships with the central and local governments on the basis of the principles of legality and the rule of law. Historically, the Ombudsman has through his case-law played a pivotal role in developing human rights guarantees in Iceland as provided for by the Constitution of the Republic of Iceland and the European Convention on Human Rights.
Professor Spano served as <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Ombudsman">Parliamentary Ombudsman</a> until 1 July 2010 when he became Dean of the Faculty of Law, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Iceland">University of Iceland</a>, but continued to serve as Ombudsman on an ad hoc basis in 2011 and 2012. On July 1st, 2012, Spano was appointed an ad hoc Judge of the EFTA Court in Luxembourg by the ESA/Court Committee. In April 2012, Spano was appointed the Icelandic Delegate to the European Committee on Crime Problems (CDCP) of the Council of Europe (COE). Also, he served as an Independent Expert to the Lanzarote Committee of the COE, set up on the basis of the European Convention on Protection for Children Against Sexual Exploitation and Sexual Abuse. On 1 March 2013, Professor Spano was again appointed <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Ombudsman">Parliamentary Ombudsman</a> on a full-time basis, serving until 1 October 2013.
Having been elected a Judge of the <a href="/wiki/European_Court_of_Human_Rights">European Court of Human Rights</a>, Judge Spano took up office in <a href="/wiki/Strasbourg">Strasbourg</a> on November 1, 2013, succeeding Professor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dav%C3%AD%C3%B0_%C3%9E%C3%B3r_Bj%C3%B6rgvinsson&action=edit&redlink=1">Davíð Þór Björgvinsson</a>, the former Judge on the Court with respect to Iceland.
-----
Writings.
Spano has written extensively in the fields of human rights law, public law, the interpretation of statutes and criminal procedural law. His scholarship has dealt mainly with the impact of the European Convention of Human Rights on domestic law, especially in relation to articles 3 and 7 of the Convention and article 4 of Protocol No. 7. In his writings on the interpretation of statutes, Professor Spano has advocated for a normative theory of textualism that views the interpretive enterprise holistically in terms of assessing the internal and external context of the statutory text.
